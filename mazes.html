Polymorph Rule Reminder:
  • In any order, each player takes a Moment or passes that turn
  • For their moment, each player explains what Action they are interested in doing
  • The Maze Controller may have that player roll or spend a resource to succeed
  • After every player has gone, The MC takes their turn. The player’s rolls in response are Saves, and do not count as Moments.

It is assumed, as professional adventurers, they come equipped with all the gear they need for the expedition.

To accomplish an action roll the specific role die of their character. If the number rolled matches one of the values in the action row, it is a success!

Rolling a 1 is only a success if with an associated Edge or Key. For instance, an Expert researching (a Books roll) or a Fighter combatting (a Blades roll) will still succeed on a 1 because it matches their core expertise. Conversely, a Tank attempting a Books roll with no academic or literacy edge will fail on a 1.

Rolling the maximum number is a Crown and can be a success, but the final outcome depends entirely on the current Darkness level and may introduce a twist or a disaster.

Actions are rolls made with a Role Die to attempt some act upon the world. Saves are rolls made with the Role die to avoid the world acting upon the character. Violent Actions cost a Heart, or a Standard Condition. The Maze Controller (Game Master) never rolls except perhaps on randomized tables.

Effect Rolls (damage, contests, or tables like Death’s Door), unlike Actions and Saves, aim for high totals rather than matching matrix values. A player may choose to use the standard die specified by a table, or substitute their personal Role Die. If using Role Die, upon rolling its maximum value, the die can explode, allowing a player to choose to roll again and add the subsequent numbers together.

An Edge is anything about a character that defines them. Most Edges are ways to gain advantage (or prevent Disadvantage). Players use Edges to explain why a character can do an Action (or would do a Save); or why they should have advantage while doing either. Players and Maze Controllers can invoke a Class or a condition in the same way that an edge would be invoked.

Conditions are binary and can only be removed through exceptional heroic effort or by performing the specific recovery action listed on the condition itself. It is not possible to have more than one of the same condition at a time. The Standard conditions (Stressed, Tired, Hurt, Wounded, Marked) can be taken instead of losing a heart from damage.

During the Epilogue, a character CLEARS all of their conditions (including Hurt, Marked, and Wounded). There is not a cost to clear Tired or Stressed or Marked during the Epilogue, but Hurt and Wounded requires spending a Treasure to clear.

Darkness Track - Tracks MC power, level dangers, and environmental hazards. 1–3: Bright Status: Safe and controlled; lamps lit. Crowns: Automatic success. Death’s Door: Advantage. 4–6: Torchlit Status: Standard adventuring state. Crowns: Negotiated success (must spend 1 Star, 1 Treasure, or make an MC deal). 7–9: Bleak Status: Dark and scary. Crowns: Automatic failure. Death’s Door: Disadvantage. 10: Nightmare Status: Triggers an unpreventable hazard event. Total blackout; moving blind forces immediate danger rolls. Crowns: Automatic failure. Death’s Door: Instant death.

Wealth Tracker (1 - 10)

Tracks the party’s fungibles used for equipment, advantage, and MC negotiations. Falling below the player count makes the party SKINT (+1 Darkness), while reaching 10 or doubling the player count (whichever is lower) makes them ENCUMBERED (+1 Darkness). While BLEAK, spending benefits only a single player. While NIGHTMARE, spending is entirely locked until all immediate threats are escaped. Fund an expedition by buying starting Treasure for 2 Wealth each, or 1 Wealth and 1 Darkness. Optionally spend a Star at any time to identify a specific treasure before returning home. During the Epilogue, roll a Role Die on the treasure table for each collected Treasure to gain the resulting Personal Wealth.

Guiding Questions
What is a maze?A maze is any adventuring situation, from dungeons, to social encounters, to overworld random hazards or enemies, where there exists a desired goal and many meaningful choices on how to end up there. This system (Polymorph) is a very rules light system that allows for much table improvization and flexibility. Answering the following questions will help anyone flesh out a Character to introduce to the story.
Why do I want to enter a Maze?
I am a undaunted by the world, curious and freeThen they are the Fool (d2 coin, 2 Hearts, 6 Stars). Driven by whims and dumb luck. With actions that are highly swingy, they can get remarkably far on fortune alone.
I am a seeker of knowledge and glory who is naturally the best at what they doThen they are the Expert (d4 die, 4 Hearts, 4 Stars). A Paragon driven by ambition and research, trading physical durability for deep insights that provide massive technical advantages.
I am a the first into danger in quest of that which I valueThen they are the Knave (d6 die, 6 Hearts, 3 Stars). Roustabouts, technicians, vanguards, scavengers, driven well-rounded, self-starters motivated to do whatever it takes to get the job finished.
I am here to stand violently against the darknessThen they are the Fighter (d8 die, 8 Hearts, 2 Stars). Combatants driven to prove themselves and smash opposing threats. They rage against the dying of the light.
I am a fearless defender that seeks to keep others safeThen they are the Tank (d10 die, 10 Hearts, 1 Stars). Juggernauts, Sentinels, Shieldwalls, driven to protect everything worth treasuring by taking the hits others cannot endure.
How do I solve problems?
By the edge of my weaponThen they have the Sword Aspect. Choose from martial classes like the Dangerous Bravo, Jaded Sellsword, Knockabout Ranger, Monster Slayer, Outcast Bugbear, Reluctant Hero, Savage Barbarian, or Valiant Dragoon. Alternate options are in the book which I encourage anyone to buy
From the security of obscurityThen they have the Shadow Aspect. Choose from stealthy classes like the Adventurous Smallfolk, Cursed Tomb Robber, Excellent Vagabond, Filthy Urchin, Nighthawk Assassin, Puzzling Locksmith, Talented Thief, or Zealous Cultist. Alternate options are in the book which I encourage anyone to buy
With power unbound by the worldThen they have the Sorcery Aspect. Choose from magical classes like the Blazing Magician, Guild Mage, Haunted Librarian, Infernal Summoner, Last Ilf, Quack Alchemist, Underground Druid, or Wise Witch. Alternate options are in the book which I encourage anyone to buy
By being different and uniqueThen they have a Select Aspect. This lets a player build a customized class, selecting a core defining Aspect, one of two optional Primary Class Traits, and one of three Attribute Edges to start.
What is my place in this world?
Three EdgesSelect a core defining Aspect, one Primary Class Trait, and one core Attribute Edge to establish baseline capabilities.
What is my drivesDetermine the internal motivation driving a character and fueling the narrative. Examples: Exploration, Greed, Ambition, Glory, Fear, Revenge.
What is my bonds?Determine the foundational relationships connecting a character to the rest of the party. Examples: Kinship, Trauma, Debt, Secrets, Rivalry, Mentorship, Shared Vice, Prophecy.
What is my best attribute?Secure the Edges of this Character by determining what they excel at and why, anchoring it to the background history that shaped that identity. This might include a previous profession (What did they do to earn coin before this) or Lineage (what are they physically, like an Human, Alp or Smallfolk) and a Community (where did they grow up and what was the culture that shaped them).
What do I look like? Appearances include lifestyle ranks, social status, superficial fashion, physical fitness, and the like. Do people see this Character as an insider or outsider of their group? Do they see them as a potential target, or someone to be avoided? Are they part of the local lineages, or exotic, strange, or frightening? Be free to imagine any origin, specialized equipment, and prepare to introduce yourself to the party. Mazes does not use encumbering shopping trips or traditional gear tracking; appearance, clothing, and starting class Kits are entirely narrative and aesthetic.
What is my name? A name goes a long way in any world. There’s the name an individual prefers to be called by, any epithets earned, any nicknames given, unrevealed legal names, true names given to us by the Gods. Mazes allows for story and mystery to evolve at the table. Keeping things consistent and being aware that anything this Character learns to do, enemies will learn to do too is the key to a fun experience. Introduce yourself to the party, and prepare for the expedition.
